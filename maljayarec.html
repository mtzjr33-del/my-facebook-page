3 Part Three: Solution Philosophy – Gradual Twinning via Qualified Inheritor

3.1 Core Idea and Strategic Logic

Instead of risking complex and costly negotiations directly with Western universities and hospitals, we adopt an intelligent and strategic model based on three main pillars:

  1. Selecting Qualified Arab "Inheritor" Partners: Arab institutions that have previously twinned with prestigious Western universities and hospitals for over 10 years
  2. Complete Package Transfer: Comprehensive transfer of leadership – management – quality – curricula – operating systems – institutional culture
  3. Use as a Strategic Bridge: Using them as a temporary bridge (5–7 years) then upgrading directly to the original Western partner after building local capacities

  • · Implementation Speed: Achieving tangible results within 24-36 months instead of 5-7 years

11.3 Similar Successful Global Models

The proposed model is based on studies and successful global models applied in several countries that achieved significant developmental leaps:

  • · Singapore Model: Benefiting from global expertise with local adaptation, focusing on quality and excellence
  • · Malaysia Model: Partnership with international institutions while preserving national identity and local values
  • · United Arab Emirates Model: Attracting international branches of global universities, while developing local institutions
  • · Qatar Model: Bringing full branches of global universities (Cornell, Georgetown, Carnegie Mellon)
  • · Saudi Arabia Model: Investing in partnerships with global institutions (KAUST with MIT, King Faisal Hospital with Mayo Clinic)

Lessons Learned from Global Models:

  • · Investment in Education and Research: Key to progress and sustainable development
  • · Smart Partnerships: Not all partnerships are beneficial, the right partner must be chosen
  • · Knowledge Transfer: The goal is not ready services but transfer of knowledge and skills
  • · Local Adaptation: Necessity of adapting global models to local environment
  • · Patience and Continuity: Results need time and continuity in application
  • · Investment in Cadres: Human element is most important in any developmental process
